Honest conclusion
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Standard-QCD aggregation gives lambda_std ~ 0.0039 (best estimate),
lambda_std ~ 0.0040 (most generous SVZ).
Required for the V2 magnetar benchmark: lambda ~ 0.879 - 1.006.
Gap: roughly two orders of magnitude.

Interpretation. The condensate-photon coupling needed for the
V2 magnetar amplification is ~10^2 above the value that
standard QCD operators (QED loop, chiral loop, VMD, magnetic
susceptibility) deliver. Two scientifically honest options
follow:

(a) Lower the V2 claim. With lambda ~ 0.01 the structural
    amplification factor is Gamma_struct ~ sqrt(1/(1 - 0.01))
    ~ 1.005, i.e. ~ 0.5 percent. NVG then contributes a small
    correction on top of the conventional dynamo/fossil-field
    mechanism rather than the dominant amplification.

(b) Keep the V2 claim, but recognise that it requires a new
    operator beyond the Standard Model QCD-photon sector --
    something like a hidden U(1) mixing with EM whose coupling
    is set by the NVG order parameter rather than by chi_QCD.
    This is allowed by the NVG framework's modified vacuum
    sector but is, strictly speaking, BSM physics in the
    gauge sector, not a derivation from standard QCD.

Either choice is publishable. Conflating them is not.
