## Overview

A phenomenological **Vacuum Mass Fraction (VMF)** model for dense nuclear matter, anchored to lattice QCD sigma terms.

~91% of the nucleon mass is not generated by the Higgs mechanism, but by nonperturbative QCD dynamics — gluon field energy, confinement, and the trace anomaly. The VMF model captures this via a single parameter:

$$f_\Omega^{(N)} = 1 - \frac{1}{M_N}\sum_{q} \sigma_{qN} \approx 0.91 \pm 0.02$$

The vacuum anchor $M_{\Omega,0} = 859 \pm 8$ MeV is **fixed by lattice QCD** and is not a free fitting parameter. This rigidly constrains the equation of state (EOS) of dense matter and yields concrete predictions for neutron star observables and heavy-ion experiments.

## Key Results

| Result | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Nonperturbative nucleon mass | $M_{\Omega,0} = 859 \pm 8$ MeV |
| Max neutron star mass | $M_{\max} \approx 2.3\,M_\odot$ |
| Radius of 1.4 $M_\odot$ star | $R_{1.4} \approx 12$ km |
| $\rho$-meson mass drop at $2n_0$ | ~24% (testable at FAIR/HADES) |
| Hyperon onset | $\sim 1.7\,n_0$ |
| Sensitivity to lattice input | $\Delta M_{\max} \approx \pm 0.05\,M_\odot$ |

## What the Model Claims

1. ~91% of the nucleon mass is nonperturbative QCD energy (confirmed by lattice data)
2. The vacuum anchor $M_{\Omega,0}$ is fixed by lattice sigma terms — not fitted
3. Causality ($c_s \leq c$) is maintained via density-dependent vector saturation
4. First-order phase transition to QGP reconciles EOS with $M_{\max} \approx 2.3\,M_\odot$
5. Concrete prediction: ~24% drop in $\rho$-meson mass at $2n_0$ (testable at FAIR/HADES)

## What the Model Does NOT Claim

- This is **not** a replacement for General Relativity
- $M_\Omega$ is **not** a scheme-independent QCD operator
- No UV completion at Planck scales is provided — this is an EFT
- The black hole interior analysis (Section 6) is **heuristic**, not a prediction
