### 3. Explaining the 1992 Gravity Shielding Experiment

The rotating YBCO disk created a weak, continuous modulation of the Cooper pair condensate at an effective frequency of ~83 Hz. This produced a static zone of reduced vacuum tension above the disk, resulting in ~2% weight loss for objects placed in that zone.

The difficulty in reproducing the result is explained by three factors:
1. **Sample dependence.** YBCO is a granular ceramic — condensate coherence depends critically on microstructure (grain boundaries, oxygen vacancies). Different samples yield radically different $\eta$.
2. **Mechanical rotation is a crude tool.** 83 Hz is negligible compared to what electronic modulation can achieve ($10^9–10^{12}$ Hz).
3. **Parasitic effects.** Mechanical rotation generates vibrations, helium convection, and electromagnetic interference that can mimic or mask the real signal.
