\begin{abstract}
\noindent
A class of cosmological models --- bounce scenarios with a density cap
near the QCD scale, cyclic models, and low-reheating histories --- never
reaches the temperatures at which the standard baryogenesis mechanisms
operate.  We quantify what remains.  For any thermal history bounded by
$T_{\max}\lesssim 130$~GeV the Standard-Model sphalerons, thermal
leptogenesis and GUT-scale mechanisms are unavailable in principle, so
the asymmetry must be produced by baryon-number-violating interactions
active at $T\le T_{\max}$.  For the representative dimension-six operator
$(qqq\ell)/\Lambda^{2}$, cosmological relevance at
$T_{\max}\sim\Lambda_{\rm QCD}$ requires
$\Lambda\lesssim 3\times10^{6}$~GeV even under a maximal thermal
chemical-potential bias (efficiency floor
$\epsilon_{\min}=1.4\times10^{-9}$), while proton stability,
$\tau_{p\to e^{+}\pi^{0}}>2.4\times10^{34}$~yr, requires
$\Lambda\gtrsim 3\times10^{16}$~GeV: the two windows are separated by ten
orders of magnitude in $\Lambda$, i.e.\ forty orders in $\tau_{p}$.  We
show that environmental ``gating'' of the operator cannot bridge the gap
--- power-law gates tied to the chiral condensate fall short by
$\sim\!20$ orders of magnitude and switch \emph{on} inside nucleons in
bag-like descriptions, while derivative (phase-velocity) gates are
radiatively unstable --- and we classify the surviving mechanisms
exhaustively: (i)~$B\!-\!L$-conserving transfer of the asymmetry to a
dark sector with kinematic nucleon protection, (ii)~condensate-carried
baryon number of the Affleck--Dine type, and (iii)~non-thermal
populations with $E\gg T_{\max}$.  Published bounce-baryogenesis
scenarios that assume equilibrium $B$-violation below the sphaleron
threshold are excluded unless recast in one of these classes.  In cyclic
bounded-$T$ models a primordial asymmetry is additionally excluded by
entropy dilution, forcing per-cycle regeneration.  As a practical
consequence, the mere coexistence of a baryon asymmetry with a stable
proton becomes a thermometer of the early universe: it implies either an
epoch above the sphaleron threshold or the existence of one of the three
structures above.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Any dynamical explanation of the baryon asymmetry of the universe,
$\eta_{B}=n_{B}/n_{\gamma}=(6.12\pm0.04)\times10^{-10}$
\cite{Planck2018}, must satisfy the three Sakharov conditions
\cite{Sakharov}.  The third condition --- baryon-number violation in
action --- is supplied in the standard scenarios either by the
electroweak sphalerons, which freeze out at
$T_{\rm sph}\simeq132$~GeV \cite{DOnofrio}, or by heavy states at the
grand-unification scale.  Both options presuppose that the universe was,
at some epoch, hot enough.

A growing class of cosmological models does not grant that
presupposition.  Non-singular bounce models in which the energy density
is capped at a microphysical scale --- in particular QCD-scale bounces
with $\rho_{c}\sim(1~\text{GeV})^{4}$, for which the maximal temperature
is $T_{\max}=(30\rho_{c}/\pi^{2}g_{*})^{1/4}\sim 400$~MeV --- never pass
through an electroweak or GUT epoch, in this aeon or, for cyclic
versions, in any aeon.  Low-reheating scenarios present the same
situation for a different reason: if the inflaton or a late modulus
reheats the universe only to $T_{\rm RH}\ll100$~GeV, the standard
mechanisms are equally out of reach; this regime has been studied since
the work of Ref.~\cite{DavidsonKolb} and remains viable down to
$T_{\rm RH}\simeq 5$~MeV.

Faced with a bounded thermal history, model builders have frequently
appealed to \emph{gravitational} or \emph{spontaneous} baryogenesis
operating near the bounce \cite{QuantumBounce,BigBounce}, following the
mechanism of Ref.~\cite{Davoudiasl}: a time-dependent background
(curvature or a rolling phase) supplies an effective chemical potential
for baryon number, and a $B$-violating interaction in equilibrium
converts it into an asymmetry that freezes out at a decoupling
temperature $T_{D}$, treated as a free parameter.  The purpose of this
paper is to point out, quantitatively, that the second ingredient is not
available for free: \emph{any} perturbative $B$-violating interaction
fast enough to matter at $T_{D}\le T_{\max}\lesssim130$~GeV makes the
proton decay at a catastrophic rate.  The qualitative tension between
low-scale $B$-violation and proton stability is old --- it was decisive,
for instance, for baryogenesis in low quantum-gravity-scale models
\cite{LowQG} --- but to our knowledge it has not been formulated as an
exclusion map for bounded thermal histories, nor confronted with the
gating strategies that a bounce environment naturally suggests, nor
combined with the entropy-dilution argument that closes the
initial-condition escape in cyclic models.

The paper is organized as follows.  Section~\ref{sec:bounded} defines
bounded thermal histories and disposes of the Standard-Model channels.
Section~\ref{sec:eft} derives the exclusion map in the
$(T_{\max},\Lambda)$ plane (Fig.~\ref{fig:excl}), whose two allowed
regions never intersect below the sphaleron threshold.
Section~\ref{sec:gates} shows that environment-dependent operator
coefficients cannot rescue the mechanism.  Section~\ref{sec:survivors}
classifies the surviving routes and gives a worked benchmark of the
dark-transfer class, including its laboratory signature.
Section~\ref{sec:cyclic} proves the corollary for cyclic models.
Section~\ref{sec:practice} collects the practical implications, and
Section~\ref{sec:conclusions} concludes.  All numerical statements are
reproducible from the public repository accompanying this paper.

\section{Bounded thermal histories}
\label{sec:bounded}

\noindent\textbf{Definition.}  A cosmology has a \emph{bounded thermal
history} if the temperature of the radiation bath satisfies
$T(t)\le T_{\max}$ for all times after the initial (or bounce) epoch,
with $T_{\max}<T_{\rm sph}\simeq132$~GeV, while remaining above the BBN
floor $T_{\max}\gtrsim5$~MeV so that standard nucleosynthesis proceeds.

For a bounce whose density is capped at $\rho_{c}$ the bound is
structural: with $g_{*}\simeq47.5$ light degrees of freedom at the QCD
scale, $\rho_{c}=(0.86~\text{GeV})^{4}$ gives $T_{\max}=432$~MeV, and in
a cyclic realization the same cap applies to every aeon.  The
Standard-Model channels then close by exponentially large margins:
\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Electroweak sphalerons.}  The vacuum transition rate is
suppressed by $e^{-E_{\rm sph}/T}$ with $E_{\rm sph}\simeq9$~TeV; at
$T=432$~MeV the exponent is $\simeq-2.1\times10^{4}$.
\item \emph{Electroweak instantons} coupled to any rolling phase
(which would violate $B+L$): the vacuum rate carries
$e^{-4\pi/\alpha_{W}}\simeq10^{-164}$.
\item \emph{Thermal leptogenesis and GUT baryogenesis} require the
thermal production of states with $M\gtrsim10^{9}$--$10^{16}$~GeV and
are unavailable by construction.  (We note in passing that the QCD
anomaly, to which a rolling strong-sector phase does couple, conserves
baryon number: $SU(3)_{c}$ is vector-like.)
\end{itemize}
Baryogenesis in a bounded history therefore requires an interaction
violating $B$ (or transferring it) that is active at
$T\le T_{\max}$.  We now show that the perturbative version of this
requirement is self-destructive.

\section{The EFT window versus proton stability}
\label{sec:eft}

The lowest-dimension baryon-number-violating operators built from
Standard-Model fields are the dimension-six four-fermion structures
$\mathcal{O}_{6}=(qqq\ell)/\Lambda^{2}$.  In a bath at temperature $T$
they mediate $\Delta B\neq0$ scatterings at a rate
$\Gamma_{B}\sim T^{5}/\Lambda^{4}$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Cosmological relevance.}
The most efficient use of such an operator in a bounded history is
spontaneous baryogenesis \cite{CohenKaplan}: a time-dependent background
provides an effective chemical potential $\mu_{B}$, and the operator
converts it into an asymmetry
$n_{B}/s\simeq\bigl(\mu_{B}T^{2}/6\bigr)/s\cdot\min(1,\Gamma_{B}/H)$.
The bias cannot exceed its thermal ceiling $\mu_{B}\lesssim\pi T$
(beyond which the linear-response expression saturates), which gives the
\emph{efficiency floor}
\begin{equation}
\epsilon_{\min}
 \;=\;\frac{\eta_{\rm obs}^{(s)}}{\eta_{\rm eq}^{\max}}
 \;=\;\frac{8.6\times10^{-11}}{45/(12\pi g_{*})}
 \;\simeq\;1.4\times10^{-9},
\qquad g_{*}=20 ,
\label{eq:eps}
\end{equation}
where $\eta^{(s)}\equiv n_{B}/s$.  Demanding
$\Gamma_{B}/H\ge\epsilon_{\min}$ at $T_{\max}$ bounds the operator scale
from above:
\begin{equation}
\Lambda\;\le\;\Lambda_{\rm viab}(T_{\max})
=\Bigl(\tfrac{T_{\max}^{3}M_{\rm Pl}}{1.66\sqrt{g_{*}}}\Bigr)^{1/4}
 \epsilon_{\min}^{-1/4}
\;=\;3.1\times10^{6}~\text{GeV}
\quad\text{at }T_{\max}=432~\text{MeV}.
\label{eq:lviab}
\end{equation}

\noindent\textbf{Proton stability.}
The same operator mediates $p\to e^{+}\pi^{0}$ with
$\tau_{p}\sim\Lambda^{4}/m_{p}^{5}$.  The Super-Kamiokande bound
$\tau_{p\to e^{+}\pi^{0}}>2.4\times10^{34}$~yr \cite{SuperK} requires
\begin{equation}
\Lambda\;\ge\;\Lambda_{p}
=\bigl(\tau_{\rm SK}\,m_{p}^{5}\bigr)^{1/4}
=3.0\times10^{16}~\text{GeV}.
\label{eq:lp}
\end{equation}

\begin{theorem}
In a cosmology with $5~\mathrm{MeV}\lesssim T_{\max}<T_{\rm sph}$, no
dimension-six baryon-number-violating operator of Standard-Model fields
can be simultaneously cosmologically relevant,
Eq.~\eqref{eq:lviab}, and consistent with proton stability,
Eq.~\eqref{eq:lp}.  At $T_{\max}=432$~MeV the two windows are separated
by a factor $10^{10}$ in $\Lambda$, i.e.\ forty orders of magnitude in
$\tau_{p}$.
\end{theorem}

Figure~\ref{fig:excl} shows the exclusion map.  Two features deserve
comment.  First, the conclusion is insensitive to the order-of-magnitude
character of the rate estimates: a generous $10^{3}$ in $\Gamma_{B}$
moves $\Lambda_{\rm viab}$ by a factor $5.6$ against a gap of
$10^{10}$.  Higher-dimension operators only widen the gap.  Second,
formally the ``EFT desert'' closes only at $T\sim9\times10^{12}$~GeV;
long before that, at $T>T_{\rm sph}$, the Standard Model supplies its
own non-perturbative $B$-violation, whose exponential activation is
precisely the kind of vacuum-safe behaviour a perturbative operator
cannot imitate (Sec.~\ref{sec:gates}).  The theorem thus \emph{recovers}
the familiar lore --- viable baryogenesis lives either at the sphalerons
or at the GUT scale --- and identifies its reason below the electroweak
scale: the intermediate range is proton-forbidden.

\section{Gating does not rescue the operator}
\label{sec:gates}

A bounce environment suggests an escape: let the operator coefficient be
large at the bounce and small in vacuum,
$\mathcal{O}_{6}\to G(x)\,\mathcal{O}_{6}$, with a gate $G$ built from
the ambient fields.  Closing a $10^{10}$ gap in $\Lambda$ requires the
vacuum amplitude suppressed by $G_{\rm vac}\lesssim10^{-22}$ relative to
the bounce.  Both natural gate families fail.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Environmental (condensate) gates.}
Take $G=(1-\sigma^{2}/\sigma_{0}^{2})^{n}$ with $\sigma$ the chiral
condensate, so that $G\to1$ where the condensate melts (the bounce) and
$G\to0$ in vacuum.  The flaw is that the proton's interior is not
vacuum: in-medium condensate reduction at nuclear density is
20--30\,\%, so the gate base inside a nucleon is
$G^{1/n}\simeq0.25$--$0.3$, and $G\le10^{-22}$ requires
$n\gtrsim37$ powers of the field --- outside any effective field
theory.  Worse, in bag-model descriptions the condensate is strongly
suppressed \emph{inside} hadrons, turning the gate fully on precisely
where it must vanish.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Derivative gates are radiatively unstable.}
Take $G=(\partial\theta)^{2}/M^{4}$ with $\theta$ a phase that rolls at
the bounce and is static today; tree-level proton decay then vanishes in
vacuum.  But no symmetry distinguishes the gated from the ungated
operator (both are invariant under the shift symmetry of $\theta$), so
vacuum fluctuations regenerate the ungated coefficient at one loop:
$\langle(\partial\theta)^{2}\rangle\sim f^{2}/16\pi^{2}$ with $f$ the
decay constant, exceeding any natural normalization
$M^{4}\sim m_{\theta}^{2}f^{2}$ by two orders of magnitude for
QCD-scale parameters.  The gate is a fine-tuning, not a mechanism.  (The
sphaleron's own exponential activation evades this argument because it
is a property of the gauge dynamics, not an operator coefficient.)

\section{The surviving mechanisms}
\label{sec:survivors}

The theorem constrains \emph{perturbative, thermal} $B$-violation.
Exactly three structures evade its assumptions.

\subsection{Class I: $B\!-\!L$-conserving dark transfer}
\label{sec:classI}

Instead of violating baryon number, transfer it.  Introduce a dark
fermion $\chi$ carrying baryon (or $B\!-\!L$) charge and the neutron
portal
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{O}_{\rm tr}=\frac{(udd)\,\chi}{\Lambda_{\rm tr}^{2}},
\end{equation}
which conserves the \emph{total} charge --- so no proton-decay operator
is generated at any order --- while violating the visible--dark
partition that a background bias can act on.  Spontaneous
\emph{cogenesis} then separates a net zero into equal and opposite
asymmetries, $N_{B}=N_{\chi}$.  Nucleon and $\chi$ stability are
guaranteed kinematically if the dark-fermion mass lies in the corridor
\begin{equation}
m_{n}-S_{n}(^{9}\mathrm{Be})\;<\;m_{\chi}\;<\;m_{p}+m_{e}
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
937.900<m_{\chi}<938.783~\text{MeV},
\label{eq:corridor}
\end{equation}
the window familiar from the neutron dark-decay literature
\cite{FornalGrinstein}: bound neutrons in the most fragile nucleus
cannot convert, and $\chi\to pe\bar\nu$ is closed.  A worked benchmark
with the bias supplied by the QCD phase (the $\eta'$ direction) at a
QCD-scale bounce gives $\Lambda_{\rm tr}\simeq1$--$2\times10^{3}$~TeV
and, unavoidably, a mixing-induced free-neutron channel
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{Br}(n\to\chi\gamma)\;\sim\;10^{-8}\text{--}10^{-6},
\end{equation}
a genuine laboratory floor: lowering $\Lambda_{\rm tr}$ to strengthen
cogenesis raises the branching as $\Lambda_{\rm tr}^{-4}$.  This class
is not hypothetical --- $B$-mesogenesis \cite{Mesogenesis,
MesogenesisCollider} realizes the same charge bookkeeping with $B$
mesons and is actively searched for at colliders; asymmetric dark
matter \cite{KLZ} is its relic-abundance-oriented relative.

\subsection{Class II: condensate-carried baryon number}

The Affleck--Dine mechanism \cite{AffleckDine} evades the theorem
because the asymmetry is generated by the coherent dynamics of a
$B$-charged scalar, not by thermal scatterings: the ``operator'' acts on
a macroscopic field displacement, and the transfer to quarks proceeds
through renormalizable couplings.  In a bounded history the usual
challenge is inverted: without a high-temperature epoch, the mechanism
must explain how the initial displacement is set --- in a bounce, e.g.\
by the contraction phase --- and why the $A$-terms do not induce
low-energy $B$-violation elsewhere.  We do not pursue this here beyond
noting that it remains logically open and requires a dedicated field
content.

\subsection{Class III: non-thermal high-energy populations}

The bath may be cold while individual quanta are not.  Three known
realizations: late decays of heavy relics (moduli reheating to
$T_{\rm RH}\sim200$~MeV with baryogenesis from the decays themselves
\cite{Moduli}); collision-induced electroweak sphalerons, where a
non-thermal particle with $E_{\rm cm}>E_{\rm sph}$ scatters off the bath
\cite{HighESph}; and evaporating primordial black holes.  All share one
requirement: a population with $E\gg T_{\max}$ must be justified within
the bounded history itself.  In one-shot low-reheating cosmologies the
inflaton or modulus supplies it naturally; in cyclic bounded models it
must be \emph{regenerated} every aeon --- see below.

\section{Corollary for cyclic cosmologies}
\label{sec:cyclic}

Cyclic models offer a tempting escape: take $\eta_{B}$ as an initial
condition inherited from before.  Entropy production closes it.

\begin{corollary}
In a cyclic cosmology whose comoving entropy grows by a factor $g>1$
per aeon while comoving baryon number is conserved, the asymmetry
dilutes as $\eta^{(s)}_{n}=\eta^{(s)}_{1}\,g^{-(n-1)}$.  For $g=2$ and
$n=77$ aeons, reproducing $\eta^{(s)}_{\rm obs}=8.6\times10^{-11}$
today requires $\eta^{(s)}_{1}\simeq6\times10^{12}$ --- more than one
baryon per entropy quantum, which is impossible.  Inheritance is
excluded by thirteen orders of magnitude; the asymmetry must be
regenerated every cycle through Class~I or~II (Class~III populations
dilute alike).
\end{corollary}

The growth factor $g$ is model-specific, but any $g\ge1.2$ excludes
inheritance over $\gtrsim150$ aeons; entropy production is the defining
feature of cyclic scenarios, so the corollary has wide reach.

The same dilution squeezes Class~III in cyclic settings.  The
collision-sphaleron route of Ref.~\cite{HighESph} requires quanta with
$E_{\rm cm}\gtrsim E_{\rm sph}\simeq9$~TeV, and the relic-decay route
requires long-lived states with $M\gg T_{\max}$; a bath capped at
$T_{\max}\sim400$~MeV cannot produce either thermally, and any
non-thermal population prepared in one aeon dilutes away like the
asymmetry itself.  A cyclic realization of Class~III therefore needs a
\emph{per-cycle accelerator} --- e.g.\ black-hole formation and
evaporation during the crunch, or the bounce dynamics itself ---
whose energetics must be demonstrated, not assumed.  Cyclic
bounded-$T$ cosmologies are thus pushed toward Classes~I and~II every
aeon, with Class~I offering the only generic laboratory handle.

\section{Practical implications}
\label{sec:practice}

\noindent\textbf{A triage rule for model builders and referees.}
Any bounce, cyclic or low-reheating proposal that explains $\eta_{B}$
must now declare which of the three surviving classes it realizes.  A
paper that posits a $B$-violating rate
$\Gamma_{B}=T^{2m+1}/M_{B}^{2m}$ with a freely chosen decoupling
temperature $T_{D}<T_{\rm sph}$ --- the standard parameterization of the
gravitational-baryogenesis literature applied to bounces
\cite{QuantumBounce,BigBounce} --- places itself inside the excluded
region of Fig.~\ref{fig:excl} unless the operator is of the
Class-I transfer type.  The figure is intended as a one-look check.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The baryon asymmetry as a thermometer.}
Two low-energy facts --- $\eta_{B}\neq0$ and $\tau_{p}>2.4\times10^{34}$
yr --- jointly imply a statement about the deep past: \emph{either} the
universe once exceeded the sphaleron threshold, \emph{or} nature
realizes one of the three structures of Sec.~\ref{sec:survivors} (a
baryon-charged dark sector, a $B$-charged condensate, or a non-thermal
high-energy population).  Bounded-history cosmologies are therefore not
unconstrained exotica: they inherit a sharp, falsifiable homework
assignment.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Experimental targets.}
The no-go channels attention toward the observables of the survivors:
the free-neutron dark branching of Class~I
($\mathrm{Br}(n\to\chi\gamma)\gtrsim10^{-8}$ at QCD-scale cogenesis ---
within two to three orders of current search sensitivity, and a
\emph{floor} rather than a ceiling), the $B$-meson signatures of
mesogenesis already pursued at $B$-factories
\cite{MesogenesisCollider}, and the flat-direction phenomenology of
Affleck--Dine sectors.  A future confirmed detection of any of these
would convert the weakest link of bounded-history cosmologies into
their anchor; continued nulls progressively close the class.

\section{Conclusions}
\label{sec:conclusions}

Cosmologies whose thermal history never exceeds the electroweak
sphaleron threshold cannot generate the baryon asymmetry through
perturbative baryon-number-violating interactions of the thermal bath:
at $T_{\max}\sim\Lambda_{\rm QCD}$ the operator scale required for
cosmological relevance and the scale required by proton stability are
separated by ten orders of magnitude, environmental gating of the
operator is either insufficient by twenty orders or radiatively
unstable, and in cyclic realizations the initial-condition escape is
closed by entropy dilution.  What survives is a short, exhaustive list
--- dark-sector transfer with kinematic protection, Affleck--Dine
condensates, non-thermal high-energy populations --- each with its own
observational handle.  The baryon asymmetry, combined with proton
stability, thereby acts as a thermometer of the maximum temperature of
the universe: whoever removes the hot epoch must add one of these three
structures, visibly.
