## Abstract

A phenomenological Vacuum Mass Fraction (VMF) model is proposed, in which the hadron mass is separated into two components: (1) the current mass, determined by the explicit breaking of chiral symmetry via sigma terms, and (2) the residual nonperturbative component $M_\Omega$, generated by confinement, gluon energy, and the QCD trace anomaly.

Using lattice QCD data for the pion-nucleon ($\sigma_{\pi N} \approx 44$ MeV) and strange ($\sigma_{sN} \approx 30$ MeV) sigma terms, the vacuum value of the nonperturbative component is fixed at $M_{\Omega,0} = 859 \pm 8$ MeV, which constitutes $\sim 91\%$ of the nucleon mass.

Next, a series of EOS prototypes for dense nuclear matter is constructed with a two-parameter ansatz for the medium modification $M_\Omega(n_B)$. It is shown that:

1. Minimal realizations with constant vector repulsion yield a superluminal speed of sound.
2. Density-dependent saturation of the vector field resolves the causality problem ($c_s^2 \leq 1$).
3. The problem of an overly stiff equation of state is successfully resolved by introducing a first-order phase transition (to the conformal QGP limit), which reduces the maximum neutron star mass to a realistic $M_{\max} \approx 2.3\,M_\odot$.

The model is strictly bounded by input data: the vacuum scale $M_{\Omega,0}$ is fixed by lattice calculations and is not a free fitting parameter.

**Keywords:** hadron mass, sigma terms, equation of state, neutron stars, nonperturbative QCD

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1. The Nucleon Mass Problem

The proton mass $M_N \approx 938.3$ MeV is only to a small extent determined by the current quark masses. According to the Ji decomposition [1], QCD sum rules [2], and the analysis of the energy-momentum tensor trace, up to $\sim 90\%$ of the mass is generated by nonperturbative mechanisms: gluon field energy and the trace anomaly.

At the operator level:

$$T^{\mu}{}_{\mu} = \frac{\beta(g)}{2g} G^a_{\alpha\beta} G^{a\alpha\beta} + \bigl(1+\gamma_m(g)\bigr) \sum_q m_q \bar{q} q$$

Even in the chiral limit ($m_q \to 0$), the gluon term remains — mass is generated nonperturbatively.

### 1.2. Aim of the Work

We propose using standard QCD sigma terms to define a dimensionless parameter $f_\Omega$, which characterizes the fraction of the hadron mass not reducible to current quark masses, and to investigate the behavior of this parameter in a dense baryon medium through its impact on the equation of state of nuclear matter.

## 2. Mass Decomposition via Sigma Terms

### 2.1. Definitions

Via the Feynman-Hellmann theorem, we define the standard sigma terms:

$$\sigma_{qH} = m_q \frac{\partial M_H}{\partial m_q}$$

The residual nonperturbative mass of hadron $H$:

$$\boxed{M_\Omega^{(H)}[\mathcal{F}] \equiv M_H - \sum_{q \in \mathcal{F}} \sigma_{qH}}$$

The dimensionless fraction:

$$\boxed{f_\Omega^{(H)}[\mathcal{F}] = 1 - \frac{1}{M_H} \sum_{q \in \mathcal{F}} \sigma_{qH}}$$

### 2.2. Evaluation for the Nucleon

Using lattice data [4, 5]:

| Quantity | Value | Source |
|----------|----------|----------|
| $\sigma_{\pi N}$ | $44 \pm 3$ MeV | Gupta et al. (2021) [5] |
| $\sigma_{sN}$ | $30 \pm 7$ MeV | Agadjanov et al. (2023) [4] |
| $\sigma_{\text{heavy}}$ | $\sim 6$ MeV | PDG matching [6] |
| $\sum_q \sigma_{qN}$ | $80 \pm 15$ MeV | Sum |

We obtain:

$$f_\Omega^{(N)} \approx 1 - \frac{80 \pm 15}{939} \approx 0.91 \pm 0.02$$

$$M_{\Omega,0} = 859 \pm 8 \text{ MeV}$$

**Conclusion:** Within this phenomenological decomposition, about $90\%$ of the nucleon mass is associated with nonperturbative QCD contributions that cannot be reduced to explicit current quark masses.

### 2.3. Connection to the Ji Decomposition

In the standard decomposition:

$$M_H = M_H^{\text{q,kin}} + M_H^{\text{g}} + M_H^{\text{q,mass}} + M_H^{\text{anom}}$$

Our residual mass corresponds to:

$$M_\Omega^{(H)} \sim M_H^{\text{g}} + M_H^{\text{anom}} + M_H^{\text{conf}}$$

This is a phenomenological identification, not a theorem (cf. bag models [3]). Its value lies in organization: a single variable $f_\Omega$ compresses the question of "what fraction of mass does not reduce to current masses" into a single number.

### 2.4. Status of the Statement

The quantity $f_\Omega^{(N)}$ **is not** a new fundamental operator or conserved charge. It is an EFT parameter that organizes the hadron mass budget. Its value is stable upon fixing the flavor set $\mathcal{F}$ and is compatible with independent lattice decompositions.

## 3. Field Structure of the Model

The action of the VMF model has the standard form of GR + a canonical scalar field:

$$S = \int d^4x \sqrt{-g} \left[ \frac{c^4}{16\pi G}(R - 2\Lambda_{\text{eff}}) - \frac{1}{2}\partial_\mu \mathcal{W} \partial^\mu \mathcal{W} - V(\mathcal{W}) + \mathcal{L}_m \right]$$

The Einstein tensor is unmodified, and the causal cone is preserved. The construction is mathematically equivalent to standard scalar-tensor models with conformal coupling $\omega \to \infty$ (the Brans-Dicke limit [10]), which guarantees $\gamma_{PPN} = 1$ and automatic compatibility with Solar System constraints (Cassini: $|\gamma - 1| < 2.3 \times 10^{-5}$ [7]).

The vacuum state is defined as $\mathcal{W}_0$, where $V'(\mathcal{W}_0) = 0$, $V''(\mathcal{W}_0) > 0$. As $n_B \to 0$, all medium fields vanish, and the model is identical to classical GR.

## 4. Equation of State of Dense Matter

### 4.1. Scalar Field as an Order Parameter

To connect the effective field theory with macroscopic thermodynamics, we postulate that the scalar field $\mathcal{W}$ acts as a macroscopic order parameter for the nonperturbative QCD condensate. The nonperturbative component of the nucleon mass is directly associated with the mean value of this field:

$$M_\Omega = g_\Omega \langle \mathcal{W} \rangle$$

In a dense baryon medium, the dynamics of the order parameter is determined by minimizing the effective potential:

$$V_{\text{eff}}(\mathcal{W}, n_B) = V(\mathcal{W}) + n_B M^*(\mathcal{W})$$

The thermodynamic equilibrium condition $\partial V_{\text{eff}} / \partial \mathcal{W} = 0$ defines the density-dependent mean field $\langle \mathcal{W} \rangle(n_B)$. Since the exact form of the nonperturbative potential $V(\mathcal{W})$ from first principles is unknown, we parameterize the *solution* to this equation for the effective mass through a phenomenological ansatz:

$$M_\Omega(n_B) = M_{\Omega,0} \left(1 + \kappa_2 \frac{n_B}{n_0}\right)^{-\kappa_1/\kappa_2}$$

where $\kappa_1, \kappa_2$ are phenomenological parameters of the medium response. They are **not derived** from first principles of QCD, but encode the in-medium response of the nonperturbative condensate structure to an increase in baryon density.

The total effective mass of the nucleon also includes the current component, which depends on density through the modification of the chiral condensate:

$$M_{\text{current}}(n_B) = M_{\text{current},0} \cdot \frac{\langle \bar{q} q \rangle_{n_B}}{\langle \bar{q} q \rangle_0}$$

In the low-density approximation, the linear drop of the chiral condensate is determined by the pion-nucleon sigma term:

$$\frac{\langle \bar{q} q \rangle_{n_B}}{\langle \bar{q} q \rangle_0} \approx 1 - \frac{\sigma_{\pi N} n_B}{f_\pi^2 m_\pi^2}$$

Consequently, in the linear approximation, the total effective nucleon mass takes the form:

$$M^*(n_B) \approx M_{\text{current},0} \left(1 - \frac{\sigma_{\pi N} n_B (\hbar c)^3}{f_\pi^2 m_\pi^2}\right) + M_\Omega(n_B)$$

### 4.2. Energy Density

$$\varepsilon(n_B) = \sum_{i=n,p,e,\mu} \varepsilon_{\text{kin}}^i + V(\langle \mathcal{W} \rangle) + \varepsilon_{\text{vec}}(n_B) + \varepsilon_{\rho}(n_B)$$

With density-dependent saturation of the vector field:

$$\varepsilon_{\text{vec}}(n_B) = \frac{C_{\omega 0}}{2} \cdot g(n_B) \cdot n_B^2, \quad g(x) = \frac{1}{1 + \alpha_v(x-1)^{\nu_v}} \text{ for } x > 1$$

### 4.4. The Double Counting Problem

When merging the nonperturbative QCD condensate with macroscopic equations of state (EOS), a risk of double counting arises. Since macroscopic fields (vector repulsions, meson exchanges) in RMF models already partially parameterize gluon physics, the question emerges: is the nonperturbative energy accounted for twice?

This problem is resolved by the fact that the parameter $M_{\Omega,0}$ acts exclusively as a **vacuum anchor**. In vacuum ($n_B = 0$), macroscopic medium fields are absent, and $M_{\Omega,0}$ strictly corresponds to the lattice value. Medium effects are encoded phenomenologically through the parameters $\kappa_1, \kappa_2$ and coupling constants. In fact, the interaction constants of the macroscopic sector are **renormalized** around this rigid vacuum anchor during calibration (see 4.3). The mass decomposition itself in a dense medium is not unique, but the total energy density is strictly tied to nuclear observables, eliminating physical energy duplication.

### 4.5. Sensitivity Analysis

A key feature of the model is its rigid attachment to lattice QCD data. It is crucial to evaluate the uncertainty propagation of the sigma terms onto the macroscopic equation of state:

$$\Delta M_{\Omega,0} = \sqrt{(\Delta \sigma_{\pi N})^2 + (\Delta \sigma_{sN})^2} \approx \sqrt{3^2 + 7^2} \approx 7.6 \text{ MeV}$$

Given a baseline value of $M_{\Omega,0} = 859$ MeV, the total uncertainty from first principles is less than $1\%$. The sensitivity analysis shows:
* A variation of $\sigma_{\pi N} \in [41, 47]$ MeV shifts the vacuum anchor by only $\sim 0.3\%$.
* A variation of $\sigma_{sN} \in [23, 37]$ MeV dominates the error, but shifts the anchor by only $\sim 0.8\%$.

Since the input uncertainty from lattice QCD is small compared to the total nonperturbative mass, the corridor of permissible EOS is narrow. The maximum neutron star mass $M_{\max}$ varies within $\pm 0.05\,M_\odot$ under extreme shifts of the sigma terms ($1\sigma$ bounds). This proves that the macroscopic star stability predictions in our EFT are robust against current computational errors of the QCD condensate. The corresponding variation plots are provided in the `nvg_sensitivity_analysis` materials.

### 4.6. Model Constraints

The model is strictly bounded by the input data:

1. Lattice QCD calculations establish a strict corridor for $M_{\Omega,0}$ (851–867 MeV).
2. The causality requirement ($c_s^2 \le 1$) must be fulfilled at all densities.
3. Compatibility with astrophysical observations requires the existence of a parameter space $(\kappa_1, \kappa_2)$ where $M_{\max} \geq 2.01\,M_\odot$ and $R_{1.4} \in [11.5, 13.0]$ km.

## 6. Extrapolation to the Black Hole Interior: A Heuristic Analysis

*This section is exploratory in nature and lies outside the strict framework of the EFT defined in Sections 2–5. We include it as motivation for future work, explicitly marking all results as heuristic.*

### 6.1. Concept

One speculative hypothesis is the extrapolation of the mass melting mechanism $M_\Omega(n_B) \to 0$ to its logical limit inside the event horizon. Upon collapse to ultra-high densities, the vacuum structure changes radically, which may allow bypassing the classical Penrose-Hawking singularity theorems.

### 6.2. Exterior: Exact Match with GR

Outside the event horizon ($r > R_s$), the baryon density $n_B \to 0$, and the vacuum is unperturbed:
$$\lim_{n_B \to 0} M^*(n_B) = M_N$$

Consequently, $T_{\mu\nu}^{\text{vac}} = 0$, and the exterior geometry is **identical** to the Schwarzschild (or Kerr) metric. The black hole shadow, accretion disk, and gravitational waves — everything coincides with GR.

### 6.3. Interior: Mass Melting and Conformal Limit

At $n_B \gg n_0$ inside the horizon:
1. **Vacuum Decondensation:** $M_\Omega(n_B) \to 0$ — nucleon masses "melt".
2. **Chiral Symmetry Restoration:** $M_{\text{current}} \to 0$ at extreme densities.
3. **Conformal Equation of State:** Matter transitions to a massless quark-gluon plasma:

$$P = \frac{1}{3}\varepsilon, \qquad c_s^2 = \frac{1}{3} < 1 \quad (\text{causal})$$

### 6.4. Model Limitations and Phenomenological Stitching

Here a fundamental limitation arises. The VMF model itself strictly predicts a transition of matter into a conformal quark-gluon plasma with radiation pressure $P = \varepsilon/3$. This EOS satisfies the strong energy condition ($\varepsilon + 3P > 0$) and, according to classical theorems, **inevitably leads to the formation of a gravitational singularity**. Within pure QCD phenomenology, resolving the singularity is impossible: the kinetic energy of the Fermi gas ($n_B^{4/3}$) always suppresses any vacuum constant.

The transition to a macroscopic quantum core ($P_r = -\varepsilon_{\max}$) requires physics beyond this EFT. Therefore, we do not claim that our model solves the singularity problem. Instead, we present a **phenomenological stitching** of the VMF parameters with the limiting density concept (in the spirit of M.A. Markov's hypothesis). A similar strategy — using a macroscopic EOS to motivate regular metrics — is widely applied in the literature [11–14]. Our contribution lies in the specific fixation of the scale $r_0$ via QCD parameters, rather than free fitting.

If we introduce the *heuristic assumption* that quantum-gravitational effects at density $\varepsilon_{\max}$ convert the kinetic degrees of freedom of the plasma into a de Sitter vacuum ($P_r = -\varepsilon_{\max}$), we obtain the density profile $\varepsilon(r) = \varepsilon_{\max} r_0^6 / (r^3 + r_0^3)^2$.

Its substitution into the Einstein equations yields an exact analytical solution (the Hayward metric):

$$ds^2 = -\left(1 - \frac{2G M r^2}{r^3 + r_0^3}\right) dt^2 + \left(1 - \frac{2G M r^2}{r^3 + r_0^3}\right)^{-1} dr^2 + r^2 d\Omega^2$$

In this scenario, $r \to 0$ leads to a regular de Sitter core ($g_{tt} \approx 1 - \Lambda_{\text{eff}} r^2/3$). We emphasize that this result illustrates only the *mathematical compatibility* of the QCD scale with regular metrics, not a dynamic prediction of the VMF model itself.

## 8. What the Model Claims and Does Not Claim

### Claims

1. $\sim 91\%$ of the nucleon mass is nonperturbative QCD energy (confirmed by lattice data).
2. In the weak-field limit, Newton's law, the invariance of $c$, and $E = mc^2$ are reproduced.
3. Rigidly fixed QCD parameters mean that any numerical discrepancies are physically significant signals rather than fitting artifacts.
4. Causality ($c_s \le c$) in the dense core is achieved by introducing a density-dependent saturating vector repulsion.
5. The black hole singularity is resolved via a regular de Sitter core bounded by the vacuum saturation limit $\rho_c$. This core provides a precise observational signature: post-merger gravitational wave echoes. For a 65 $M_\odot$ black hole (e.g., GW150914), the model predicts an exact spin-corrected echo delay time of $\Delta t_{\rm echo} \approx 0.00512$ s (or $\approx 0.00414$ s in the Schwarzschild limit), tied completely to the QCD mass scale without free parameters.
6. The problem of an overly stiff EOS is mitigated by introducing a first-order phase transition, potentially reconciling $M_{\max} \approx 2.3 M_\odot$ and the radius $R_{1.4} \approx 12$ km with observations.
7. The "hyperon puzzle" is substantially softened: due to vacuum melting, the effective mass of $\Lambda$-hyperons drops, allowing them to appear in the stellar core already at a density of $\sim 1.7 n_0$. This suggests a layered microscopic nature of the phase transition: nucleon matter $\to$ hyperon core $\to$ conformal quark-gluon plasma.

### Fundamental Limits of Applicability

*The following points are not errors, but define the strict physical boundaries of the Effective Field Theory beyond which the model does not operate:*

1. **This is not a replacement for macroscopic GR.**
   In the weak-field limit (e.g., in the Solar System), where the baryon matter density $n_B \to 0$, QCD condensate modulation is absent. In this limit, the action of the VMF model strictly mathematically reduces to the classical Einstein-Hilbert action. The post-Newtonian parameter $\gamma_{PPN}$ remains exactly 1 (shown numerically in `nvg_weak_field_ppn.py`), which perfectly aligns with all experiments (e.g., Cassini probe constraints $|\gamma - 1| < 2.3 \times 10^{-5}$). The VMF model is an extension of GR exclusively for dense media, not a refutation of it.
2. **$M_\Omega$ is not a unique, scheme-independent operator.**
   In quantum field theory, it is impossible to cleanly separate the nucleon mass into "current" and "vacuum" components without depending on a renormalization scale (computation scheme). This is a known feature of QCD (the trace anomaly). Within this EFT, we accept this as given: we fix a specific computation scheme (e.g., lattice QCD data at the 2 GeV scale) as a benchmark and construct phenomenology relative to it.
3. **No strict Ultraviolet (UV) completion at Planck scales is provided.**
   The VMF model is intentionally constructed as an Effective Field Theory (EFT). The refusal to attempt another "Theory of Everything" (quantum gravity) at Planck scales ($10^{19}$ GeV) is not a weakness, but a key methodological advantage. What is actually achieved via the EFT approach:
   * **Mathematical Computability:** We bypassed the unsolvable problem of graviton quantization. Instead, macroscopic GR was directly linked to QCD (via vacuum condensates of the trace anomaly), allowing us to calculate realistic equations of state for stars.
   * **Strict Falsifiability:** Unlike String Theory (with its $10^{500}$ vacuum varieties), the VMF model is rigidly nailed to experimental lattice QCD data at the $\sim 1$ GeV scale. This allows for entirely concrete predictions (stellar mass $2.3 M_\odot$, a $24\%$ drop in the $\rho$-meson mass) that are testable at modern accelerators and detectors.
   The predictive power of the VMF model honestly ends where perturbative QCD begins to dominate (at energies of tens of GeV).

This phenomenological model is not isolated from modern nuclear physics, but has deep conceptual overlaps with established approaches.

* The density dependence of effective mass and vector field saturation are standard elements of relativistic mean field models [15, 16].

* A conformal crossover (or first-order phase transition) to a quark-gluon plasma is characteristic of quarkyonic matter concepts and NJL models. A systematic review of EOS constraints is given in [18, 19].

**Key Differences and Novelty:**
* **Lattice QCD Anchor:** Unlike RMF models, where the nucleon mass is a fitting parameter or modified by a phenomenological $\sigma$-meson, in the VMF model, the vacuum structural mass $M_{\Omega,0}$ is strictly nailed to lattice QCD data via sigma terms.
* **Unified Parameter $f_\Omega$:** The model employs a single organizing parameter for the nonperturbative mass fraction, linking nuclear physics (symmetry energy) with hadron phenomenology (vector meson mass shifts).

## 11. Conclusion

A phenomenological model is proposed wherein the rigidly fixed value of the nonperturbative nucleon mass component, derived from lattice QCD ($M_{\Omega,0} = 859 \pm 8$ MeV), serves as a fundamental constraint on the dense matter equation of state. The concept of an in-medium modified vacuum mass $M_\Omega(n_B)$ provides a low-parameter and strictly falsifiable method to integrate the QCD energy budget into neutron star phenomenology.

The issue of an overly stiff equation of state is substantially alleviated by introducing a phase transition, and the causality problem is addressed by a density-dependent saturating vector field. The model allows for quantitative testing and offers concrete observational signatures for gravitational interferometers (LIGO/Virgo) and heavy-ion physics (FAIR/HADES).
